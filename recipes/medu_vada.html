Medu-Vada

Info

Medu Vada, the crispy and fluffy South Indian savory donut made with urad dal. It's a classic dish served with coconut chutney and sambar.

🍩 Medu Vada Recipe (South Indian Style)

🧂 Ingredients:

For the Batter:

  • Urad dal (split black gram, skinless) - 1 cup
  • Water - as needed (very little, to grind)
  • Salt - to taste

For Flavoring:

  • Black pepper (whole or crushed) - ½ tsp (optional)
  • Cumin seeds - 1 tsp
  • Ginger - 1 tsp (finely chopped)
  • Green chilies - 1-2 (finely chopped)
  • Curry leaves - 1 sprig (chopped)
  • Onion - 1 small (finely chopped, optional)
  • Hing (asafoetida) - a pinch (optional)

For Frying:

  • Oil - for deep frying

👩‍🍳 How to Make Medu Vada

🔸 1. Soak & Grind Dal

Rinse 1 cup urad dal thoroughly and soak it in water for 4-6 hours or overnight.

Drain water completely and grind the dal to a smooth, thick batter. Use very little water — just enough to help it grind. (A wet grinder works best, but a blender can do too.)

🔸 2. Whip Batter

Whip the batter with a spoon or hand to make it light and fluffy. This helps vadas become soft inside and crispy outside.

🔸 3. Mix Spices

Add chopped green chilies, ginger, curry leaves, cumin seeds, black pepper, and salt. You can add onions too if desired.

Mix gently.

🔸 4. Shape Vadas

Wet your palm or a plastic sheet. Take a small amount of batter, shape it into a ball, and press slightly to flatten.

Make a hole in the center with your finger (like a donut).

🔸 5. Deep Fry

Heat oil in a deep kadai or pan.

When oil is medium-hot (not smoking), carefully slide vadas into the oil.

🔸 6. Drain & Serve

Remove and drain on paper towels.

🍛 Serving Suggestions:

Serve hot Medu Vadas with:

  • Coconut chutney 🥥
  • Sambar 🍲
  • Sambar 🍲

🔧 Tips:

  • Batter should be thick — if too runny, vadas will soak oil and not hold shape.
  • To test batter: drop a little in water — it should float.
  • You can use a banana leaf, zip-lock bag, or parchment to shape the vadas.
